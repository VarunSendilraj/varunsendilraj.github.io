beliefs

personal beliefs and perspectives that shape how i see the world. these evolve as i grow, but here's where i stand today.

01

dont chase fame, chase mastery

going throught life, i've noticed two kinds of people—those chasing status, and those chasing depth.

the ones i admire most aren't optimizing for the next big opportunity. they're obsessed with the craft, driven by curiosity, not recognition.

in the end, i belive that the pursuit of mastery—not fame—will take people the furthest.

02

always work backwards

when i'm stuck, i've learned to step back and work in reverse.

sometimes the clearest path forward is found by imagining how i'd fail—then doing the opposite.

03

welcome faliure

an expert in any field is just someone who's failed in more ways than most around them.

this can be in software development where someone who has made the most mistakes in the past but now knows the ins and outs of the problem.

or a comedian who failed 100 open mic nights but iterates util he gets the audience gasping for air.

this idea of faliure is something people fear but i belive it can be the best way to optimize for learning if you are willing to learn from it.

04

take risks early

comming close to graduation, i've been thinking a lot about what comes next—big tech, grad school, maybe starting something on my own.

most people would choose the safe option, the one that will give them the dopamine hit in the short term.

but when ur young, you have the most time to learn and make mistakes with little to no consequences.

so take the risks early, and live life like a main character in a an underdog story.

05

work hard, and dont forget to show off

i've never had trouble putting in the work. but after spending time in big tech, startups, academia, and side projects, i've realized something:

the difference between the top 1% and the top 0.01% often comes down to how well they communicate and share their work.

people won't dig to find out what you've built—it's on you to show them. that's still something i struggle with, but i'm learning it's part of the job.

06

go on more walks

walks in nature have become my form of meditation. some of my best thoughts and reflections come when i'm just walking—no music, just me and my thoughts (sometimes speaking out loud).

it's how i process everything going on in my head.

this is what grounds me. find your own version of that—and do it often. it brings surprising clarity.

07

never ask for permission, ask forgiveness

in school, we’re taught to follow rules and wait our turn. but that’s not how the real world works.

if you really want something, you have to go after it—relentlessly, unapologetically.

and if it causes problems, most people will forgive you—especially if you acted in good faith.

sometimes, for fast progress, you have to start by bending the rules a little.
