thoughts

personal beliefs and perspectives that shape how i see the world. these evolve as i grow, but here's where i stand today.

01

dont chase fame, chase mastery

at a school filled with top engineering talent, i've noticed two kinds of people—those chasing clout, and those chasing depth.

the ones i admire most aren't optimizing for the next big opportunity. they're obsessed with the craft, driven by curiosity, not recognition.

in the end, it's the pursuit of mastery—not fame—that takes them the furthest.

02

work backwards

when i'm stuck, i've learned to step back and work in reverse.

sometimes the clearest path forward is found by imagining how i'd fail—then doing the opposite.

03

chase faliure

an expert in any field is just someone who's failed in more ways than most around them.

this thought helps me welcome failure instead of fearing it. each mistake teaches me something i couldn't have learned otherwise—something i'll carry with me for a lifetime.

04

take unconventional paths

as i get closer to graduation, i've been thinking a lot about what comes next—big tech, grad school, maybe starting something on my own.

one thing that's helped is thinking of life like a story: what choice would make it more interesting?

sometimes the less obvious path—if it's a calculated risk—ends up being the one that brings the most fulfillment.

05

work hard, and dont forget to show off

i've never had trouble putting in the work. but after spending time in big tech, startups, academia, and side projects, i've realized something:

the difference between the top 1% and the top 0.01% often comes down to how well they communicate and share their work.

people won't dig to find out what you've built—it's on you to show them. that's still something i struggle with, especially with the fear of judgment. but i'm learning it's part of the job.

06

go on more walks

walks in nature have become my form of meditation. some of my best thoughts and reflections come when i'm just walking—no music, just me and my thoughts (sometimes out loud).

it's how i process everything swirling in my head.

this is what grounds me. find your own version of that—and do it often. it brings surprising clarity.

07

never ask for permission, ask forgiveness

in school, we’re taught to follow rules and wait our turn. but that’s not how the real world works.

if you really want something, you have to go after it—relentlessly, unapologetically.

and if it doesn't go as planned, most people will forgive you—especially if you acted in good faith.

sometimes, progress starts by bending the rules a little.
